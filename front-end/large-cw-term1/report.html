State 3 things that have inspired you when creating your website. These could include guest speakers, other websites, artists, developers etc.

I didn't really take a lot of inspiration from others. I just thought that making a website as my own personal portfolio could be very beneficial to my future. For the content I like the design of linkedin which I probably took inspiration from.

State 3 ways in which your site is accessible.

My website is accessible as I provide alternatives for auditory and visual content, provide ways to help users navigate and find content and I ensure my website is easily readable and understandable.

State 3 ways in which you have considered usability in your site.

My site is responsive to different devices, it offers a clear simple design and lastly is relevant to the user's needs.

State 3 things you had to learn/find out by yourself when creating your site, and describe how you did that (i.e. what searches you did, any new tools/techniques you learned, any changes/adaptations you made to a particular resource to make it work with your site).

Although I knew about Angular JS I hadn't previously used it much so there was a bit of a catch up to get it up and running. I also learnt about bootstrap more along with css styling design.

The reason I liked this was because it allowed for animation to be used and other techniques with events I could use in the future.
